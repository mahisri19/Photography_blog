      Abstract Photography


Abstract photography, sometimes called non-objective, experimental or conceptual
photography,is a means of depicting a visual image that does not have an immediate
association with the object world and that has been created through the use of
photographic equipment, processes or materials. An abstract photograph may isolate
a fragment of a natural scene in order to remove its inherent context from the viewer
it may be purposely staged to create a seemingly unreal appearance from real objects,
or it may involve the use of color, light, shadow,texture,shape and/or form to convey
a feeling, sensation or impression. The image is produced using traditional photographic
equipment like a camera, darkroom or computer, or it may created without using a camera
by manipulating film, paper or photographic media, including digital presentations.
