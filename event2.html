NGEE ANN PHOTOGRAPHIC EXHIBITION

Goh Koon Peng, 66, a Hainanese, was born in Singapore in 1955 and devoted himself to photography for 28 years. Goh's photography is an expression of the realism of the subjects with a humanitarian touch. A master of light, colour and composition, he is able to capture the perfect moment in a specific time, space of people or scenery without much post-editing. He thrives to show the most authentic side of the subjects and the holistic beauty between humans and their living environment. In this exhibition, Goh will be featuring 20 photographs, entitled “A New Departure from the Heart”. This series of photographs was taken between 2007 and 2021 in Singapore and various countries and showcases different aspects of humanitarian and their environment.


3F

The 3F Photography Exhibition is a highly anticipated annual event that celebrates the art of photography, showcasing the works of talented photographers from various backgrounds and styles. This exhibition is known for its focus on three key elements: Form, Frame, and Focus. Form: The exhibition explores the artistic forms and compositions captured by photographers. It highlights the visual elements, such as lines, shapes, textures, and patterns, that create a strong visual impact in the photographs. From abstract compositions to carefully arranged still lifes, the Form element emphasizes the artistic interpretation of the subject matter. Frame: The Frame element of the exhibition emphasizes the power of framing within photography. Photographers skillfully use framing techniques to guide the viewer's eye, create depth, and draw attention to specific elements within the image. Whether through the use of natural frames or deliberate framing choices, the photographers in this exhibition showcase their ability to capture compelling compositions within the frame.


AUSTRALIAN MUSEUM

Featuring 100 photographs by 74 photographers, the exhibition showcases an amazing array of animals and landscapes from the bioregion of Australia, New Zealand, Antarctica and the New Guinea. The images highlight the skill and timing of wildlife photographers, who venture into remote landscapes, often in extreme temperatures and brutal conditions, to capture their award-winning shots. This year, seven young photographers are celebrated in the Junior category. The connection between animals and humans – sometimes destructive, sometimes companionable – is explored in the portraits of the Urban Animals, Threatened Species and Our Impact categories.
