PARISPHOTO

ParisPhoto is an esteemed international photography exhibition held annually in the vibrant city of Paris, France. Recognized as one of the world's premier photography events, ParisPhoto attracts renowned photographers, collectors, curators, and art enthusiasts from around the globe. The exhibition serves as a dynamic platform for photographers to showcase their works and engage with a diverse and discerning audience. It features an extensive range of photographic genres, including fine art, documentary, fashion, conceptual, and experimental photography. The carefully curated selection of images offers a captivating exploration of visual storytelling and artistic expression. ParisPhoto provides a unique opportunity for visitors to immerse themselves in the world of photography. The exhibition showcases the works of both established masters and emerging talents, reflecting the evolving landscape of contemporary photography. Through the lens of these photographers, visitors can discover new perspectives, narratives, and social commentary, as well as witness the beauty and power of the medium itself


ECE GOKALP'S EXHIBITION

Ece Gökalp's exhibition in Foam 3h revolves around two major events that took place in the Northeast of Turkey. With this project, Gökalp sheds light on conflicts related to cultural heritage, ecological devastation, and the complexities of changing geography due to historical events through in-depth research, photography, and archival material.Gökalp's main interest is the theory of photography. She explores the controversy surrounding photography, the photographic gaze, and the indexicality of photography in various contexts. She also investigates how photography can shape our perception of different places. Ece Gökalp is a visual artist from Istanbul. She studied photography and video at YTU in Istanbul and spent a semester at the Graphic Design Department of the Royal Academy of Arts in The Hague with an Erasmus scholarship. Later, she went to Berlin to study Art in Context at UdK Berlin.The exhibition of Ece Gökalp is part of Kısmet, a multi-year project that was created by artist Ahmet Polat and further developed in collaboration with Foam. It was initiated based on the longstanding cultural, diplomatic and economic connections between Turkey and the Netherlands. Through research, outreach, and exhibitions, Kısmet focuses on the complex and rich perspectives of Turkish visual culture, as seen through the perspective of different generations of image makers.


THE FAMILY OF MAN

The Family of Man is a legendary and influential photography exhibition that was curated by Edward Steichen and first displayed at the Museum of Modern Art (MoMA) in New York City in 1955. Considered a groundbreaking showcase, it is renowned for its universal theme of humanity, celebrating the shared experiences and emotions that connect people across cultures and borders. The exhibition featured photographs from over 60 countries, contributed by hundreds of photographers, both established and emerging. The images depicted various aspects of human life, including birth, love, work, family, conflict, and joy. The exhibition aimed to emphasize the fundamental similarities that exist among all individuals, transcending differences in nationality, race, or religion. The Family of Man sought to promote the idea of global unity, understanding, and peace through the power of photography. The curated collection of photographs revealed the universality of human experiences, illustrating the triumphs, struggles, and daily routines that shape our lives.
